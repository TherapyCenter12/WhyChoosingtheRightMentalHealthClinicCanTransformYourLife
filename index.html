FAQs

1. What services does a mental health clinic typically offer?

A good mental health clinic provides a wide range of services depending on your needs — from individual therapy, trauma and PTSD counseling (including EMDR trauma sessions), to couples & family therapy, behavioral therapy for ADHD, treatment for anxiety and depression, psychiatric evaluations and medication management, and even virtual/tele-therapy appointments. Many clinics also offer holistic emotional-wellness support and personalized mental health plans.

2. How do I know if the therapist or counselor at the clinic is qualified and right for me?

It’s important to check that the mental health professionals are licensed and experienced — for example, licensed mental health counselors or psychiatrists. Before starting, ask about their training, their experience with issues similar to yours (e.g. trauma, mood disorders, ADHD), their therapy style, how they track progress, and what approach they use (talk therapy, EMDR, behavioral therapy, medication management, etc.). Mental Health America +2 inclusivetherapists.com +2 Also, many clinics offer an initial consultation (sometimes a short call or trial session) so you can see if you feel comfortable with them.

3. What should I expect during my first visit to a mental health clinic?

On your first visit, the therapist or psychiatrist will likely ask about your history — your symptoms, how long you’ve experienced them, major stressors or traumatic events, physical or mental health history, any medications/supplements you take, and how the symptoms impact your daily life.
